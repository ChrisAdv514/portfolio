A bit About Me

I'm a data scientist with a physics background, currently completing an MSc in Data Analysis and Machine Statistical Learning at the University of Crete and FORTH. My work focuses on applying transformer architectures and language-model style methods to problems in cheminformatics and drug discovery, especially molecular property prediction.

I started in AI through physics-driven computation, first experimenting with sequence models for music generation, and then moving deeper into machine learning. Recently I've been focusing more on the chemistry and biology side, building models and pipelines to predict molecular properties and support early-stage drug discovery workflows.

Beyond my academic work, I'm actively involved in the Erasmus Student Network as a Web Project Administrator, where I helped develop the award-winning Accommodation Map recognized as the most innovative digital tool improving ESN services in Greece.

I've also participated in summer schools and hackathons, including the Onassis Foundation Summer School and Innodays Hackathon, where I built prototypes for health and well-being challenges under tight deadlines.

Comprehensive natural language processing research exploring transformer architectures and their applications in text understanding tasks.

  • Developed custom attention mechanisms for improved context understanding
